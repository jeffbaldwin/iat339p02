New Pin : Sad Songs

2.2.2017

We are welcoming the arrival of Stay Home Club and their awesome new pin design that owns the slogan “Sad Songs”. Stay Home Club is created and ran by Olivia Mew from Montreal, Canada. She designs for those with no life but is unapologetic about it.

Sad Songs was a statement piece created to signify sensitivities associated with emotional songs and being okay with those feelings. When coming up with an idea, Olivia was listening to her “Breakup not Makeup” Spotify playlist, a playlist she made after she broke up with her boyfriend. She felt ... SHOW MORE surge of emotions that gathered in her entire body and she knew the number of sensitive individuals that could empathize with her current state.

The pin is designed and made with care and quality. Although the pin is only available for a limited time only, we hope Stay Home Club will make this their second home.

W-h-hat? Mixed Emotions Club?

11.29.2016

If this is your first time hearing about the Mixed Emotions Club, let us tell you what it is all about.

Created by the Sensitivity Charity and Tuesday Bassen, we’ve put together the Mixed Emotions Club, a club where the members never meet up but connect through the spirit of the pins. The goal of the club is to take a stand on being expressive and confident whatever the emotion is.

How to Join the Mixed Emotions Club (3 Ways):
1. Purchase a Mixed Emotions Club pin
2. Visit TuesdayBassen.com for more ... SHOW MORE Mixed Emotions Club product
3. Send us a video to list reasons you want to join

Once you are part of the club, you will have a lifetime membership and a potential chance in meeting Tuesday Bassen at one of our workshops! There is no additional membership fee.

Designer : Tuesday Bassen

10.15.2016

Who is Tuesday Bassen and what are all these amazing pins she has designed?

Meet Tuesday Bassen, the multi-talented freelance artist. She’s an award-winning illustrator, art director, and designer coming from sunny Los Angeles, California with clients such as Lucky Peach, The New Yorker and United Nations.

Tuesday has always been a creative that we drew inspiration from. She is a proud feminist and frequently looks to strong female personalities as muses for her works. Unafraid of exploring ideas that challenge gender roles and traditional values ... SHOW MORE, she believes people are just individuals with regular emotions. We contacted Tuesday in regards to designing for us and after a coffee shop conversation, we immediately knew we would be creating awesome things.

Our ongoing collaborations with Tuesday has allowed both of us to mature and strengthen our knowledge in emotional and mental health. Stay tune for a new pin design very soon!
